Action Center

It's important to keep our oceans free from any garbage. Here are some steps YOU can take to reduce the amount of pollution in our oceans.

Volunteer

Volunteering is an important step towards of reducing pollution. When you volunteer, you are helping maintain and protect our oceans while regenerating populations of fragile ecyosystems affected by ocean pollution. Volunteering also allows you to see your community in a different perspective through working with others. There are differnt types of volunteer work to create cleaner oceans.

  • River Cleanup

    Rivers and streams provide 65% of United States drinking water. When there is trash and pollution in those rivers, it can contaminate our water source and the ecosystems inhabitated there. Click here to find a river cleanup near you!

  • Coastal Cleanup

    Coastal cleanups improve the coastal and ocean ecosystems. By removing litter that has the harmful potential to disrupt the aquatic enviroment there, it increases the quality of the coast. Click here to find a coastal cleanup near you!

    If your located in California, there is a California Coastal Cleanup Day on September 17, 2022! Learn more here.

  • Ocean Cleanup

    Ocean cleanups can dramatically reduce the concentration of plastic in the ocean garbage patches. They are vital to mitigate the ocean pollution. Click here to find an ocean cleanup near you!

Sustainable Seafood

If you're a lover of seafood, there are ways you can eat it in an enviromentally friendly way! Sustainable seafood is seafood that is caught or farmed in ways that consider the long-term vitality of harvested species and the well-being of the oceans, as well as the livelihoods of fisheries-dependent communities.

Donate

Donating is another way to have a direct impact on reducing our oceans pollution. The websites listed below are great organizations we recommend you donating to or even researching about!

  • Ocean Conservancy was founded in 1972. Their mission is to protect the ocean from todayâ€™s greatest global threats. They create evidence-based solutions for a healthy ocean and enviroments for the wildlife that depend on it. Ocean Conservancy invests in research, leadership and advocacy that advances science-based solutions that reduces pollution in our oceans. Their International Costal Cleanup, which has been going on for 30 years, works with millions of volunteers of all ages around the world! Learn more about Ocean Conservancy here.

  • Blue Marine Foundation was founded in 2010. This ocean conservation charity is dedicated to restoring the ocean to health by addressing overfishing. Blue Marine Foundation recognizes that overfishing is a major threat for the food security. This is because overfishing takes precious life that belongs in the ocean. As a result, there is a decrease in production of oxygen, absorption of carbon dioxide. Learn more about Blue Marine Foundation here.

  • Oceana is the largest international organization focused on protecting the world's ocean and was founded in 2001. They seek direct change by influencing specific policy decisions on a national level in order to preserve and restore biodiversity in our oceans. They strive for control by winning policies in countries all over the world. Oceana has curretly protected 1.2 million square miles of ocean. Learn more about Oceana here.

  • The Ocean Cleanup was founded in 2013. They create and develop technologies that clear the world's oceans of plastics. They came to the conslusion that most of the plastic that enters the ocaeans originates from rivers primarily. The Ocean Cleanup strives to intercept plastics found in rivers and cleaning up whats already floating in oceans. By removing plastic in its orginal size, it will help prevent the debris from becoming any smaller and turning into dangerous mircroplastics. Learn more about The Ocean Cleanup here.
